Handicrafts of India

Indian handicrafts represent centuries of skill, regional identity, and cultural heritage. From rare tribal traditions to globally recognized crafts, each form reflects the creativity of Indian artisans.

Rare & Endangered Handicrafts

Rogan Art

Rogan Art is one of the rarest textile arts in India, created using thick paint made from castor oil. Practiced by only a few families, it is known for its symmetry and vibrant motifs.

Toda Embroidery

Toda embroidery is a tribal art practiced by the Toda community of the Nilgiri hills. It uses red and black geometric patterns symbolizing nature and community life.

Dhokra Metal Casting

Dhokra is an ancient metal casting technique using the lost-wax method. It is practiced by tribal communities in central and eastern India and dates back over 4,000 years.

Traditional Regional Handicrafts

Wooden Handicrafts of India

Wooden handicrafts are among the oldest Indian crafts, showcasing traditional carving skills passed down through generations.

Blue Pottery

Jaipur’s blue pottery is a Persian-influenced ceramic art recognized for its blue dye, floral patterns, and glossy finish.

Famous & Widely Recognized Handicrafts

Jute Handicrafts of India

Jute handicrafts represent eco-friendly Indian craftsmanship using natural fibers blending sustainability with traditional design.

Banarasi Silk Weaving

Banarasi silk sarees are among the most famous Indian textiles, known for their gold and silver brocade work and rich designs.
