Music of India

Indian music is a deeply rooted cultural tradition combining melody, rhythm, emotion, and spirituality. It is broadly classified into classical, semi-classical, folk, and modern forms.

Classical Music of India

Hindustani Classical Music

Hindustani music developed in North India and is based on raga (melodic framework) and tala (rhythmic cycle). It emphasizes improvisation and expressive performance.

Carnatic Classical Music

Carnatic music originated in South India and focuses on structured compositions. It is devotional in nature and features kritis composed by saint-composers.

Semi-Classical Music

Thumri

Thumri is a semi-classical vocal form emphasizing emotion, romance, and devotion.

Bhajan

Bhajans are devotional songs expressing love and surrender to the divine.

Qawwali

Qawwali is a Sufi devotional music form known for its spiritual intensity and powerful vocals.

Folk Music of India

Baul Music

Baul music is a mystical folk tradition emphasizing spiritual freedom and philosophy.

Bihu Music

Bihu music accompanies the Bihu dance and celebrates harvest and joy in Assam.

Rajasthani Folk Music

Rajasthani folk music narrates heroic tales and desert life through song.

Modern & Contemporary Indian Music

Indi-pop

Indi-pop combines Indian melodies with contemporary global music styles.

Fusion Music

Fusion music blends Indian classical or folk music with Western genres.

Indian Musical Instruments

Sitar

The sitar is a plucked string instrument widely used in Hindustani classical music.

Veena

Veena is one of the oldest Indian string instruments and is central to Carnatic music.

Tabla

Tabla is a pair of hand-played drums providing rhythmic accompaniment in classical music.

Mridangam

Mridangam is the primary percussion instrument in Carnatic classical music.

Bansuri

Bansuri is a bamboo flute associated with Indian classical and devotional music.

Shehnai

Shehnai is a traditional wind instrument commonly played during weddings and ceremonies.

Sarangi

Sarangi is a bowed string instrument widely used in Hindustani classical music. It is renowned for its ability to closely imitate the nuances of the human voice.

Pakhawaj

Pakhawaj is a traditional percussion instrument mainly used in Dhrupad performances. It produces deep, resonant rhythms and predates the modern Tabla.
